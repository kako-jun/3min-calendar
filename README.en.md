## FAQ

### Q. Can I use it without internet?

**Yes, you can.**

Once you open the page, it works offline.

Your data is saved on your phone (or computer).

### Q. Where is my calendar saved?

It's automatically saved in your browser (Chrome, Safari, etc.).

Even if you close the app, you can continue editing when you open it again.

### Q. Can other people see my data?

**No.**

Data is stored only on your phone (or computer).

It is never uploaded to the internet.

### Q. Will I lose my data if I change phones?

**Yes.**

Data doesn't transfer automatically to a new phone.

To transfer data, go to Settings ‚Üí "Data Management" ‚Üí "Export" to save a backup file, then "Import" it on your new phone.

### Q. What is "Share screen"? Is it safe?

Don't worry. This is a feature to save the calendar as an image.

Your screen is not being shared with anyone.

When you tap "Allow", it captures only the calendar area and saves it as an image.

### Q. How do I post to Instagram or LINE?

1. Tap the **üì§ Share** button
2. Allow the "Share screen" permission
3. Select "Instagram" or "LINE"
4. Post as usual

If it doesn't work, use **üíæ Save** to save the image, then post it the same way you normally share photos.

### Q. What is Rokuyo?

Rokuyo are traditional Japanese lucky/unlucky days like "Taian" (lucky) and "Butsumetsu" (unlucky).

In Japan, some people prefer certain days for weddings or avoid certain days for funerals.

This is useful if you want to tell customers "Today is a lucky day!"

Turn it off if you don't need it.
